# Hiring Standards

## 1. Competency Framework

The source of truth for all career step evaluations is your organization's **Competency Framework**.

**Requirement**: Agents MUST read the full competency document and extract the behaviors specifically for the candidate's **Target Career Step** (e.g., Junior, Mid, Senior, Staff). Do not rely on summaries.

## 2. Organization Context

Evaluation must be benchmarked against the organization's environment:

| Factor | Considerations |
|--------|----------------|
| **Scale** | Employee count, geographic distribution, external contractors |
| **Culture** | Modern/high-paced vs. traditional; agile vs. waterfall |
| **Complexity** | Cross-functional and cross-country stakeholder management |

## 3. "One Team" Cultural Rubric (Example)

Adapt these pillars to your organization's values:

| Pillar | What to Look For | Red Flags |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| **Aligned Autonomy** | Acts without permission, owns problems end-to-end | Waits for instructions, blame-shifts |
| **Clarity** | Writes documents/RFCs, makes work visible | "Code explains itself" attitude |
| **V1 Over V-None** | Ships imperfect solutions to unblock progress | Perfectionism, analysis paralysis |
| **Collaboration** | Seeks feedback early, low ego | Lone wolf, defensive |

## 4. Protocol Fidelity

- **InterviewCraft**: Must use CoT reasoning and provide mandatory [Evaluation Guides] for every question.
- **Hiring Analyst**: Must perform forensic analysis and cite the competency framework for every observation.
- **Manager Responsibilities**: Gather JD, CV, and Transcript, then hand them to the Agent. The Agent is responsible for reading the Competency Framework directly from the source.
